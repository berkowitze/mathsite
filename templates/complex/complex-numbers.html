Complex Numbers

Complex numbers are points in the $z$ plane. These points are represented as usual, $z_0=(x_0,y_0)$. However, complex numbers have several properties that make working in the complex plane advantageous in many situations.
The first of these properties is the multiplication of complex numbers.
The product of two points in the complex plane, $z_1 = (x_1, y_1)$ and $z_2 = (x_2, y_2)$, is defined to be: $$z_1 z_2 = (x_1 x_2 - y_1 y_2, x_1 y_2 + x_2 y_1)$$ This is the base assumption of the complex plane, and it is what leads to the use of complex numbers. If we represent the plane as a real axis and an imaginary axis, points $z_0 = (x, y)$ can be equivalently represented $z_0 = x + iy$, which has real part $\re(z_0)=x$ and imaginary part $\im(z_0) =y$.

We can easily see that this representation of points fulfills the definition of multiplication above: $$(x_1 + iy_1)(x_2 + iy_2) = (x_1 x_2 - y_1 y_2 + i(x_1 y_2 + x_2 y_1)) = (x_1 x_2 - y_1 y_2, x_1 y_2 + x_2 y_1)$$ This uses the usual rule that $i^2 = -1$.
